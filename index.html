Summary

This is a blog which talks about the time I went with Warwick in Africa to teach in South Africa. This includes a great range of things from the challenges I faced to the lessons I learnt as well as taught. Take a scroll down to discover more... Or click back to blogs (top right) to go back.

The First Day

This was the first day at our school... and I thought South Africa was hot!

New challenges

There were many challenges I faced in the classroom. Language barriers, covering lessons, different abilities, resources and attendance were among many. Respect was fortunately not one of these challenges though. This picture is the view from the school building, thought to be one of the best in Stellenbosch.

Seeing South Africa

Table mountain and Lions head. This spectacular scenery took my breath away.

New people

We were a multinational team with students from the University of Singapore and Warwick!

The display

I made each of the three classes I taught a notice board. I then had a game each lesson and the winner got to put their name on a star and pin it on their notice board. The kids loved it!

Culture lessons

It was a great opportunity when a student teacher at the university of stellenbosch had their placement at our school. He taught me many things about the culture and this helped in getting to know the students better. We met many other nationalities too. Ernst and young volunteers, a German teacher who was on an overseas placement as part of his teacher training and an American volunteer working at our school to improve the library.

A legacy

I wanted to give my teacher as much help as possible to continue the activities we had been doing. One of these activities was doing a daily "speed test" to improve mental maths. I left an egg timer and about 500 tests for her to use.

The last day

This was my last day teaching. This picture is full of the students' energy and happiness.

URSS

"Research is formalised curiosity. It is poking and prying with a purpose." - Zora Neale Hurston

Currently, I am undertaking an 8 week long research project with Dr Vicky Henderson as part of the URSS scheme at the University of Warwick. The subject of my research is:

Executives commonly receive stock options which can be exercised - that is, given the right to buy stock of their company for a fixed price at a time they choose. Typically, they build up a portfolio of options over their tenure with a company. This research looks at the optimal timing decision, the manager faces, of when to exercise options given they can exert managerial effort to influence the underlying stock price. Second Year Essay

"Some mathematical topics, circulant matrices, in particular, are pure gems that cry out to be admired and studied with different techniques or perspectives in mind." - [8]

The essay is aimed at second year mathematics students and covers the basic properties of circulant matrices, including an explicit formula for the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of any circulant matrix and a brief introduction to the discrete Fourier transform. The essay then goes on to discuss two applications. Firstly, we use the properties of circulant matrices to give a unified approach to solving polynomials and secondly, we discuss the role of circulant matrices in solving particular systems of linear equations.

What lead to me discovering circulant matrices? After greatly enjoying exercises involving special kinds of matrices, I was intrigued. I wanted to find out what other types of matrices were out there and what properties they could have.

Research Project

"At a purely formal level, one could call probability theory the study of measure spaces with total measure one, but that would be like calling number theory the study of strings of digits which terminate." - Terence Tao

This report is concerned with the generalisation of the ergodic theorems for actions of free groups. In particular, it will discuss the example, established by Terence Tao [17], of a measure preserving system and an L1 function for which the sequence of averaging operators is unbounded in n for almost every x.
